IMPORTANT INFORMATION REGARDING NOTARY PUBLICS


In situations where an associate works remotely, it is acceptable for a notary public to complete Section 2 of the I-9 FORM for the employer. Federal Law stipulates that the person who views an employee’s employment authorization documents should also complete and sign Section 2. Notary publics are commissioned by states to perform duties such as verify signatures and administer oaths. They can be found at banks, law firms and financial institutions.

After the associate has completed Section 1 and the top portio of Section 2,the notary should do the following:

  • Sign, stamp and date each copy of the supporting documents after examining original documentation and meeting the associate in person. The stamp should include the notary’s name, commissioning state and commission expiry.
  • Complete the CERTIFICATION portion of Section 2 of the I-9 Form after completing the step above.

The following is a link to the USCIS Web page that contains information on using notary publics to verify forms: About Form I-9, Employment Eligibility Verification.(http://www.uscis.gov/files/form/m-274.pdf) . We suggest that the associate print this page and bring it with him/her in the event that the notary is not aware that the notary can verify work eligibility with respect to I-9s.
